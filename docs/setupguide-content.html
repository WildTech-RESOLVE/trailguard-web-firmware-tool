01What's in the box

Unpack and confirm all components before heading into the field. Your TrailGuard kit includes the camera unit, power, connectivity, and mounting hardware shown below.

  • TrailGuard camera & communications unit
  • 32 GB microSD card
  • 3.7V rechargeable Li-ion battery
  • Battery charger
  • Cellular antenna ×2
  • Mounting bracket & zip ties
  • Setup Mode magnet
  • SD card removal tweezer
  • Extra screws & silica gel pack

02Component overview

The camera and communications unit are camouflaged to enhance concealment in the field. Familiarize yourself with each labelled component before installation.

03Powering up

1

Open the communications unit

Unscrew and lift the cover to expose the SIM slot, battery bay, and antenna connectors.

2

Insert the SIM card

Slide the activated SIM into the holder until it seats fully. Ensure it has an active data plan for real-time transmission.

3

Tighten the antenna

Hand-tighten both cellular antennas onto the SMA connectors. A loose antenna degrades signal and transmission reliability.

4

Connect the battery

Align the connector orientation with the tab, then push down fully to secure the connection. The unit powers on immediately.

5

Close up

Refit the cover and tighten all screws to maintain the weatherproof seal.

04Triggering the camera

Once the battery is connected, movement in front of the motion sensor triggers the camera. Each trigger captures four consecutive images, which the onboard AI evaluates instantly.

Blue — no detection
AI found no target of interest. The camera returns to standby.
Green — positive detection
One of the images is transmitted by the communications unit.

05Real-time transmission

Only images with a positive AI detection are transmitted. Transmission typically takes under 20 seconds, following this LED sequence:

  1. 1Red and green lights on
  2. 2Amber light on — connecting to cell network
  3. 3Amber light flashing — transmission in progress
  4. 4LEDs off — transmission complete

06Battery & SD card

Battery charging

  • Fully charge the 3.7V Li-ion battery before deployment (~8 hours).
  • A full charge supports around 10,000 transmissions.
  • Battery status is sent with every alert and shown on the dashboard.
  • Range: 4.2V (100%) to 3.25V (low).
Full charge Charging

Accessing the SD card

All full-resolution images are saved to the microSD card. Carefully unscrew the cover and retrieve the card with the provided tweezer.

  • Images are auto-sorted by AI into folders (people, animal, vehicle).
  • Each event includes a metadata text file — detection probability, battery charge, storage.
  • Always replace the card and screw the cover closed tightly.

07Setup Mode

Setup Mode lets you connect to the device wirelessly (over-the-air) from a phone or laptop to edit configurations and run updates.

1. Enter Setup Mode

Insert the magnet (stored on the mounting bracket) into the side slot. Power on the unit and trigger the motion sensor — the blue LED turns on.

Blue LED solid — Setup Mode active

2. Connect over Wi-Fi

Join the access point TrailGuard-XXXX (XXXX = last 4 digits of IMEI; no password). Open a browser to 192.168.1.1 to reach the Main Menu.

The Setup Mode interface

Device Tests — Camera Preview

Livestreams the camera frame to verify placement, coverage, and avoid blind spots during installation.

Device Tests — Cellular Module

Verifies connectivity and data transmission with live logs: network status, APN, config & firmware download, live capture upload.

Firmware

Update device firmware by manual file upload.

Settings

Camera info, APN, detector transmission rules, updates, factory reset. Press Load Current Settings before editing to preserve current values.

08Nightjar IQ Dashboard

All TrailGuard alerts are sent to the online dashboard at app.nightjar.tech. Dedicated accounts can be set up per organization, team, or location and linked to specific cameras.

  • Sort and view alert images by location, detection type, and date/time.
  • Review "Periodic Health Check" images and monitor camera status (e.g. battery charge) in the field.
  • Perform basic data analysis across your deployment.

09Deployment tips

Placement

  • Deploy on trails with trees or terrain on both sides — avoid pointing at open areas.
  • Install several meters back from the trail to optimize field of view and object distance.
  • Where possible, elevate the camera several meters above ground.

Why elevate?

  • Better field of view — AI detection is agnostic to viewing angle and stays accurate.
  • Improved concealment from trail traffic.
  • Stronger signal reception and transmission.
  • Remove branches and objects that could falsely trigger the sensor.

Use Live View to preview and adjust the angle, then walk in front of the installed camera to confirm a proper trigger (check the LED).
